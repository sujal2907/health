Child Care

1stmonth

Breastfeeding Technique:

Positioning:

Sit in a comfortable chair with good back support or lie down in a side-lying position. Hold your baby close to your body, supporting their neck and shoulders with one hand and their bottom with the other.

Breastfeeding Technique:

Sit in a comfortable chair with good back support or lie down in a side-lying position. Hold your baby close to your body, supporting their neck and shoulders with one hand and their bottom with the other.
Ensure your baby's mouth covers a large portion of the areola (the dark area around the nipple) rather than just the nipple itself. Wait for your baby to open their mouth wide before bringing them to the breast to ensure a deep latch.
Babies may nurse for varying lengths of time. Allow your baby to nurse as long as they want on the first breast before offering the other breast. Look for signs of active sucking, swallowing, and occasional pauses during feeding sessions, indicating effective milk transfer.
Newborns have small stomachs and need to feed frequently, typically 8-12 times in a 24-hour period. Watch for hunger cues such as rooting, lip-smacking, sucking on fists, or turning their head towards your breast.
Expect to feed your newborn throughout the night, as they have shorter sleep cycles and frequent waking periods. Keep your baby close to your bed in a bassinet or crib for easy access during nighttime feedings.
Additional Point:- In the early days, it can be helpful to keep a record of your baby's feeding times and diaper changes to ensure they're getting enough milk and gaining weight adequately.

Hygiene:

Start with sponge baths until the umbilical cord stump falls off. Use this time to bond with your baby by talking or singing softly. Once the umbilical cord stump falls off, transition to regular baths in a baby tub or sink. Ensure the water temperature is warm but not too hot, and support your baby's head and neck throughout.
Keep diaper-changing essentials handy in a designated area to streamline the process. Use diaper-changing time as an opportunity for interaction and bonding by talking or making eye contact with your baby.
Dress your baby in comfortable, practical clothing that's easy to put on and take off. Opt for soft fabrics that won't irritate their delicate skin.

Physical Growth

Schedule a visit to your pediatrician within the first week after your baby's birth for a comprehensive physical examination, weight check, and assessment of general health.
Many hospitals offer newborn hearing screening before discharge, but if your baby didn't have one, schedule a hearing screening within the first month.

2ndmonth

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding on demand or formula feeding every 2-3 hours, totaling about 8-12 feedings per day. Ensure proper latch and feeding technique to prevent nipple soreness or feeding difficulties.

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding on demand or formula feeding every 2-3 hours, totaling about 8-12 feedings per day. Ensure proper latch and feeding technique to prevent nipple soreness or feeding difficulties. Look for hunger cues such as sucking on fists, rooting, or fussiness, and feed your baby accordingly

Hygiene:

Give your baby a sponge bath 2-3 times per week using warm water and a mild, fragrance-free baby soap or cleanser. Support your baby's head and neck while bathing and ensure the room is comfortably warm to prevent chilling.
Change your baby's diaper frequently, at least every 2-3 hours or whenever it's wet or soiled. Clean the diaper area gently with baby wipes or warm water and cotton balls, wiping from front to back to prevent infections.
Dress your baby in comfortable, soft clothing made of breathable fabrics like cotton. Consider layering with a onesie or bodysuit and a light jacket or sweater to adjust for changes in temperature. Avoid overdressing or using clothing with strings, ribbons, or loose buttons that could pose a choking hazard.

Physical Growth

Follow the recommended vaccination schedule provided by your pediatrician to protect your baby from diseases like Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP), Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib), and Polio, among others. Keep track of vaccination appointments and ensure your baby receives all necessary shots.
Additional Tips:- Incorporate tummy time into your baby's daily routine to help strengthen their neck, shoulder, and arm muscles and promote motor development. Start with short sessions of tummy time several times a day, gradually increasing duration as your baby becomes more comfortable.

3rdmonth

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding on demand or formula feeding every 2-4 hours, totaling about 6-8 feedings per day.
Offer breast milk or formula as the primary source of nutrition, and avoid introducing solid foods until around 6 months of age, unless advised otherwise by your pediatrician.

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding on demand or formula feeding every 2-4 hours, totaling about 6-8 feedings per day.
Offer breast milk or formula as the primary source of nutrition, and avoid introducing solid foods until around 6 months of age, unless advised otherwise by your pediatrician. Hold your baby in an upright position while bottle-feeding, and allow them to feed at their own pace, stopping for burping as needed.

Hygiene:

Give your baby a bath 2-3 times per week using warm water and a mild, fragrance-free baby soap or cleanser. Support your baby's head and neck while bathing, and use a soft washcloth to gently clean their body, avoiding the eyes and ears.
Change your baby's diaper frequently, at least every 3-4 hours or whenever it's wet or soiled. Use baby wipes or warm water and cotton balls to clean the diaper area, wiping from front to back for girls to prevent infections.
Dress your baby in comfortable, breathable clothing appropriate for the season. Opt for soft fabrics like cotton and avoid tight or restrictive clothing. Layering with a onesie or bodysuit and a light jacket or sweater is ideal for adjusting to changes in temperature.

Physical Growth:

Your pediatrician may perform routine screenings for eye and hearing problems during well-baby visits. Notify your doctor if you notice any signs of vision or hearing impairment, such as not responding to visual stimuli or not turning toward sounds. Follow the recommended vaccination schedule provided by your pediatrician to protect your baby from diseases like Rotavirus, Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP), and Pneumococcal disease, among others. Keep track of vaccination appointments and ensure your baby receives all necessary shots.
Continue to support your baby's head and neck when holding them, especially during the first few months when their neck muscles are still developing. Use one hand to support the baby's head and the other to support their bottom or back.
Engage in frequent skin-to-skin contact and cuddling sessions to promote bonding and emotional security. Talk, sing, and read to your baby to stimulate their language development and cognitive skills. Tips: Incorporate regular tummy time sessions into your baby's daily routine to help strengthen their neck, shoulder, and arm muscles and promote motor development. Start with short sessions several times a day, gradually increasing duration as your baby becomes more comfortable. Babywearing: Consider using a baby carrier or sling to keep your baby close while keeping your hands free for daily tasks. Ensure the carrier provides proper support for your baby's head and neck.

4thmonth

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding or formula feeding every 3-4 hours, totaling about 5-6 feedings per day. If breastfeeding, your milk supply may start to regulate, and you may notice longer gaps between feedings.

Breastfeeding:

Continue breastfeeding or formula feeding every 3-4 hours, totaling about 5-6 feedings per day. If breastfeeding, your milk supply may start to regulate, and you may notice longer gaps between feedings. Ensure your baby is still feeding enough throughout the day.
If formula feeding, follow the recommended amount as per your pediatrician's advice.

Hygiene:

Give your baby a bath 2-3 times per week using warm water and a mild, fragrance-free baby soap or cleanser. You can increase the frequency if your baby enjoys baths or if they get dirty frequently. Support your baby's head and neck while bathing, and use a soft washcloth to gently clean their body, paying extra attention to folds and creases.
Change your baby's diaper regularly, at least every 3-4 hours or whenever it's wet or soiled. Use baby wipes or warm water and cotton balls to clean the diaper area thoroughly, wiping from front to back for girls to prevent infections.
Dress your baby in comfortable, breathable clothing appropriate for the weather. Opt for soft fabrics like cotton and choose outfits that allow for easy movement. Consider dressing your baby in layers to adjust for changes in temperature, and avoid overdressing to prevent overheating.
